The execution layer connects approved trade intent to Interactive Brokers while preserving broker abstraction and paper/live separation.

execution detailsORDER BUS

Execution should be predictable: it receives an approved order request, validates mode and connectivity, constructs broker-specific orders, and reports the result back to monitoring.

FeatureBreakdown
IBKR integrationUses Interactive Brokers Gateway or TWS through an async adapter such as ib_async.
Paper/live modesConfiguration decides whether the order is simulated, sent to paper trading, or routed live.
Order typesSupports market orders first, with limit and stop variants available as execution policy evolves.
Bracket ordersCan send entry, take-profit, and stop-loss legs together so protection is attached immediately.

Execution Contract

approved_trade -> broker_adapter.connect() -> build_order() -> place_order() -> confirm_or_alert()

The broker adapter pattern keeps the door open for future integrations without forcing the strategy, resolver, or risk layers to change.
