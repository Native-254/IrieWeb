Risk Management

Risk rules determine whether a trade is allowed, how large it can be, where it stops out, and when the whole bot should slow down or halt.

The risk manager is the main safety gate between signal generation and execution. It turns theoretical entries into account-aware position sizes and rejects trades that would overextend the portfolio.

ControlPurpose
Kelly criterionSizes positions from estimated edge and win/loss characteristics, usually capped to a fractional Kelly value to reduce volatility.
Portfolio heatLimits total open risk across all active trades so several correlated losses cannot overwhelm the account.
Exposure limitsCaps gross and per-symbol exposure, preventing accidental concentration or leverage creep.
Daily loss limitStops new trading after a configured daily drawdown threshold is reached.
Max drawdownReduces size or halts trading after equity falls too far from its high-water mark.
ATR stopsPlaces initial and trailing stops based on volatility instead of arbitrary fixed distances.

Approval Pipeline

signal -> proposed trade -> Kelly size -> heat check -> exposure check -> ATR stop -> approved order

This gives the bot room to trade, but only inside boundaries that are visible, configurable, and testable.
